## Aaron Fletcher — The Fletcher Method

**Who:** Aaron Fletcher, founder of Fletcher Method, Inc. Spent ~15 years working with large Silicon Valley brands (Nolo, Caring) and small business marketing before launching Fletcher Method in 2012.

**Framework:** 9-step online marketing system organized in three phases. Over 6,000 entrepreneurs have used it to launch and scale online businesses.

### The 9 Steps in 3 Phases

| Phase | Theme | # | Step | What it does |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Foundation** | Anonymous → Intimate (customer + market) | 1 | Avatar | Find the ideal customer for your business |
| | | 2 | Message | Million Dollar Message — condense everything into one powerful sentence |
| | | 3 | Offer | Product Ladder — map pricing across products and programs |
| **Sales Funnel** | Leaky Bucket → Airtight | 4 | Signature Solution | World-class visual representation of your unique process |
| | | 5 | Swim Lanes Sales Funnel | "5 Minute Funnel Finder" to choose the right funnel |
| | | 6 | Ultimate Lead Magnet | Single educational piece that turns strangers into subscribers |
| **Traffic** | Slot Machine → ATM | 7 | Facebook Ads Floodgates | Define one paid traffic source with positive ROI |
| | | 8 | Publishing Powerhouse | 6-step content marketing process |
| | | 9 | Ranking Rulebook | SEO — invisible to unmissable |

### Key Concepts

- **Frameworks as currency**: "An ever-growing repository of simple frameworks, or one page 'cheat sheets' that cut out 95% of the time and frustration."
- **Sequence matters**: "The power of frameworks lies in completing them accurately and in order."
- **Inner sub-frameworks**: Each of the 9 steps contains its own internal framework (e.g., Step 2 contains the Million Dollar Message; Step 3 contains the Product Ladder).
- **Authority Amplifier**: Critical bonus framework that bridges Lead Magnet to conversion event.

## Ryan Deiss — Customer Value Journey

**Who:** Founder of DigitalMarketer. Creator of "Customer Value Optimization" methodology. Inspired in part by Desmond Morris (who had 12 stages for human courtship); Deiss condensed it to 8 for marketing.

**Framework:** An 8-stage framework for moving customers "from a less desirable BEFORE state to a more desirable AFTER state."

### The 8 Stages

| # | Stage | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Aware** | They first discover your brand — ads, blogs, social posts, word-of-mouth |
| 2 | **Engage** | Build connection through helpful content — emotional involvement |
| 3 | **Subscribe** | Trade something valuable for their email |
| 4 | **Convert** | Small first action — trial, sample, entry offer ($8–$20 to cover acquisition cost) |
| 5 | **Excite** | Dopamine hit from buying — give them a memorable experience, quick wins |
| 6 | **Ascend** | Move them up the value ladder to higher-priced offers — this is where real profit happens |
| 7 | **Advocate** | Ask for reviews, testimonials, passive promotion |
| 8 | **Promote** | Active promotion — affiliate, referral, true belief |

### Phase Grouping

DigitalMarketer typically groups the 8 stages as:

- **Acquire** (Aware, Engage, Subscribe)
- **Monetize** (Convert, Excite)
- **Maximize** (Ascend, Advocate, Promote)

### Key Quote

> "No matter what you're selling, your product or service is a vehicle that transports people from a less desirable before state to a more desirable after state."

## Russell Brunson — Value Ladder

**Who:** Founder of ClickFunnels. Direct-response marketer before ClickFunnels (supplements, software, info products). Author of *DotCom Secrets* (2015), which introduced the Value Ladder publicly.

**Framework:** A pricing and offer architecture, not a customer journey. Customers ascend a sequence of offers from low-risk entry points to high-ticket continuity.

### The 5 Rungs

| Rung | Name | Price Range | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Bait** | Free–$7 | Lead magnets, tripwires. Acquisition and trust-building, no profit |
| 2 | **Frontend** | $100–$2,000 | Your core product — main transformation, convert browsers to customers |
| 3 | **Backend** | $2,000–$25,000 | Coaching programs, live events |
| 4 | **Summit** | $25,000+ | Inner Circle, mastermind, top-tier exclusivity |
| 5 | **Continuity** | Recurring | Subscription, membership, maintenance — long-term lifetime value |

### ClickFunnels' Own Value Ladder Example

- Bait: Free book (DotCom Secrets) — $7.95 shipping
- Frontend: ClickFunnels software ($97–$297/month)
- Backend: Funnel Hacking Live ticket ($997), Two Comma Club Coaching ($10K–$25K)
- Summit: Inner Circle Mastermind ($50K/year)

### Key Quote

> "If someone doesn't have an actual Value Ladder, they don't have a business; they are just selling a product."

## Alex Hormozi — $100M Leads + Money Models

**Who:** Founder of Acquisition.com. Author of *$100M Offers*, *$100M Leads*, and *$100M Money Models*. Built and scaled multiple businesses to nine figures.

### $100M Leads Framework

A lead is anyone you can reach out to. The goal: **interested leads** who are likely to buy.

**The Core Four — Four Ways to Get Leads:**
1. Warm outreach
2. Cold outreach
3. Free content (posting)
4. Paid ads

**Customer Journey (3 stages):**
1. **Attract** — Lead Magnet that solves a small but significant problem
2. **Engage** — Hook-Retain-Reward system to keep them moving
3. **Scale** — Leverage (customers, employees, agencies, affiliates do it for you)

### $100M Money Models Framework

Three stages for cash flow optimization:

1. **Get Cash** — Attraction Offer that gets new customers for less
2. **Get More Cash** — Upsells and downsells to make more money faster
3. **Get the Most Cash** — Continuity offers (subscriptions, recurring revenue) for max LTV

### Key Concepts

- **Rule of 100**: Do 100 marketing actions every day for 100 consecutive days
- **Constraint analysis**: Find where leads drop off in the funnel; small improvements at the constraint = big revenue impact
- **Lead Magnet quality**: Solve a small but significant problem completely

### Key Quote (paraphrased)

> "Going from 100 leads a day to 20,000 leads a day comes from leverage."

## Donald Miller — StoryBrand 7-Part Framework

**Who:** Author of *Building a StoryBrand*. Founder of StoryBrand and Business Made Simple. Built the framework on the hero's journey used in classic literature and film.

**Framework:** A messaging structure, not a sales pipeline. Makes the customer the hero of the story and your brand the guide.

### The 7 Parts

| # | Part | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Character** | The hero is your customer, not your brand. Understand what they want |
| 2 | **Problem** | External (surface), Internal (feeling), Philosophical ("this shouldn't be this hard") |
| 3 | **Guide** | You are the guide — express empathy and authority |
| 4 | **Plan** | Simple 3-step plan that makes saying yes easy |
| 5 | **Call to Action** | Primary (direct: "Schedule a Call") + Secondary (transitional: "Download the Guide") |
| 6 | **Avoid Failure** | Stakes — what they lose if they don't act |
| 7 | **Success** | Paint the after-picture — what life looks like solved |

### Key Quote

> "Never assume people understand how your brand can change their lives. Tell them."

### Paradigm Shift

The customer is the hero. Your brand is the guide. Most brands position themselves as the hero, which is a major mistake.

## Chet Holmes — Buyer's Pyramid + Stadium Pitch

**Who:** Author of *The Ultimate Sales Machine*. Trained Fortune 500 sales teams. His daughter Amanda Holmes (CEO of Chet Holmes International) updated the framework with the Inverted Pyramid.

### The Buyer's Pyramid

At any given moment, in any market:

| % | State |
|---|---|
| **3%** | Buying now |
| **7%** | Open to buying |
| **30%** | Not thinking about it |
| **30%** | Don't think they're interested |
| **30%** | Definitely not interested |

This data was empirically derived: Chet polled audiences of thousands at live events.

### The Stadium Pitch

Imagine all your leads in a stadium. If you talk about your product/service, **90% would leave**. The Stadium Pitch is the strategy to get the **entire pyramid** to stay and listen — by leading with **education-based marketing**, not sales pitches.

Example transformation:
- ❌ "The 5 ways our office equipment can benefit you" — appeals to 10%
- ✅ "The 5 ways you're wasting money in operations and administration" — keeps the whole stadium

### The Inverted Buyer's Pyramid (Updated)

In the new edition of *The Ultimate Sales Machine*, Amanda Holmes introduced an evolved framework:

1. **Global Pain** — Universal pain that catches the entire pyramid (e.g., "85% of businesses were in financial struggle last year")
2. **Targeted Pain** — Then narrow to your specific problem domain

### Red Ocean / Blue Ocean

- **Red Ocean** = the 3% buying now (everyone fighting for them)
- **Blue Ocean** = the 97% who are latent (open to education)

## Jay Abraham — Three Ways to Grow a Business

**Who:** One of the most renowned business strategists. Framework first articulated in the 1980s. Underpins many modern coaching/consultancy frameworks.

**Framework:** Every business grows in exactly three ways. Pull all three levers and growth becomes **geometric**, not linear.

### The 3 Pillars

| # | Lever | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Increase the number of clients** | New customer acquisition |
| 2 | **Increase the average transaction value** | Get each customer to buy more per checkout |
| 3 | **Increase the frequency of purchases** | Get existing customers to buy more often |

### The Geometric Growth Principle

> "Do any one and you can grow in a linear manner... But when you leverage all 3 at the same time, the business growth will be explosive."

If you improve each pillar by 10%, the combined effect is multiplicative, not additive.

### Why It Matters

Most businesses obsess over Pillar 1 (new clients) — the most expensive and least leveraged path. The breakthrough is dedicating equal strategic attention to Pillars 2 and 3.

## Frank Kern — Behavioral Dynamic Response

**Who:** Direct-response marketer. Creator of Behavioral Dynamic Response (BDR) and Intent Based Branding (IBB).

### Behavioral Dynamic Response (BDR)

An automated marketing method that **customizes messaging based on the prospect's behavior**, instead of putting everyone on the same conveyor belt.

**How it works:**
- Track every prospect interaction (clicks, video watches, opens)
- Send customized follow-ups based on **response indicators**
- Anticipate the "no" — multi-phased campaigns assume rejection and overcome it

### Key Principles

- **Profit from the No**: Many prospects won't buy first time. Use trust-building over time.
- **Response Indicators**: Specific actions (started video, finished video, clicked link) that tell you where they are in the journey.
- **Customized Messaging**: Each behavior triggers a different, targeted follow-up.

### Intent Based Branding (IBB)

A method to make prospects **know, like, and trust** your business before you ever try to sell. Trust is the bridge between product and customer.

### The 4-Stage Trust Cycle

Know → Like → Trust → Buy

## Sam Ovens — Consulting Accelerator

**Who:** Founder of Consulting.com. Built one of the largest consulting training programs in the world.

### Two Main Programs

**Consulting Accelerator** (foundational): 6-week program to build a six-figure consulting business from scratch. Promises first client within 42 days.

**UpLevel Consulting** (scaling): For consultants going from six to seven figures.

### Core Philosophy — Three Pillars

1. Rewire your brain — be a new person
2. Find a niche and offer that provides real value
3. Build a proven, repeatable process to attract/convert clients

### Client Acquisition Framework (5 Modules)

1. **Lead Generation** (Automated) — sales funnels, lead magnets
2. **Webinar Funnel** (Nurture) — webinars warm them up for a sales conversation
3. **Sales Process** (Conversion) — streamline lead-to-client
4. **Optimization** (Measurement) — data and analytics
5. **Strategic High-Ticket Acquisition** (Scaling) — long-term contracts, relationships

### Key Concept

> "Predictable and controllable system for getting more clients."

## Industry Frameworks (non-coach)

### Sandler Selling System (7 Phases — "Sandler Submarine")

1. Bonding and rapport
2. Up-front contract (set expectations)
3. Pain (identify pain points)
4. Budget (discover budget)
5. Decision (learn the decision-making process)
6. Solution (propose)
7. Post-Sale (next steps)

**Key concept:** Sandler flips the traditional model — the buyer pursues the deal, not the seller. Strong focus on qualifying *out* quickly via the "pain funnel."

### MEDDIC / MEDDPICC

A qualification checklist (not a stage sequence) for enterprise deals:

- **M**etrics
- **E**conomic Buyer
- **D**ecision Criteria
- **D**ecision Process
- **I**dentify Pain
- **C**hampion
- **P**aper Process (added in MEDDPICC)
- **C**ompetition (added in MEDDPICC)

**Best for:** Enterprise B2B with deal sizes above $50K, complex buying committees, sales cycles exceeding 90 days.

### Challenger Sale (5 Phases)

From *The Challenger Sale* by Matt Dixon and Brent Adamson. Insight-led selling for competitive markets.

1. Warm-up
2. Reframe
3. Rational drowning
4. Emotional impact
5. Solution

**Five rep profiles**: Hard worker, relationship builder, lone wolf, problem solver, **Challenger** (most effective).

### HubSpot Flywheel (3 Phases)

Replaces the funnel metaphor with a compounding wheel:

- **Attract** — turn strangers into visitors
- **Engage** — turn visitors into leads, leads into customers
- **Delight** — turn customers into promoters

### Universal CRM Lifecycle (Salesforce / HubSpot / Pipedrive)

The standard record-type sequence across all major CRM platforms:

**Lead → Opportunity → Customer**

These are not phase names — they're the actual record types salespeople work with daily.

### Standard B2B Sales Pipeline (9 stages)

The most universally accepted B2B pipeline:

Prospecting → Qualification → Discovery → Demo/Presentation → Proposal → Negotiation → Closing (Won/Lost) → Implementation → Retention/Expansion

## Field Observations

### 1. Only Deiss built a literal stage sequence

Of all 7 coaches, only **Ryan Deiss** published a stage-by-stage customer journey with named customer states (Aware → Promote). The others built different shapes:

- **Brunson** built a *pricing/offer architecture* (Value Ladder rungs)
- **Hormozi** built *lead acquisition + money model systems*
- **Miller** built a *messaging framework* (StoryBrand)
- **Holmes** built a *market awareness model* (Buyer's Pyramid)
- **Abraham** built *growth levers* (3 ways)
- **Kern** built a *behavioral nurture methodology* (BDR)
- **Ovens** built a *coaching curriculum* (modules for the consultant)

### 2. Consumer info-product orientation

Most of these frameworks were built for **consumer info-products** — courses, books, coaching programs, software. They assume short sales cycles, single decision-makers, fixed-price offers, and a "click and buy" conversion event.

### 3. No coach in this group built a B2B contract-signing stage

Consumer info-product journeys assume *Convert = transaction = done*. There is no separation between verbal commitment and a signed contract because consumers don't sign contracts to buy a $97 product. None of these coaches designed for that gap.

### 4. No coach in this group built a scoping stage

Consumer products are pre-built. There is no negotiated scope. Brunson's Frontend is the closest, but it's a fixed price-point offer, not custom-scoped work.

### 5. Post-sale is where most coaches expand

Deiss splits post-sale into 3 stages (Ascend → Advocate → Promote). Brunson splits it into 3 rungs (Backend → Summit → Continuity). Hormozi has Get More Cash → Get the Most Cash. They all see post-sale as where the real money is, and they all break it into multiple stages.

### 6. The 3-phase shape is universal

Almost every coach uses some version of this 3-phase grouping:

- **Phase 1**: Get them in
- **Phase 2**: Get them to buy
- **Phase 3**: Keep them buying / buying more

The names differ:

- Acquire / Monetize / Maximize (Deiss)
- Attract / Engage / Scale (Hormozi)
- Get Cash / More Cash / Most Cash (Hormozi Money)
- Bait / Frontend / Backend-Continuity (Brunson)
- More Clients / Bigger Transactions / More Frequency (Abraham)
- Attract / Engage / Delight (HubSpot Flywheel)

### 7. The Fletcher pattern is the deepest structural innovation

Fletcher's 9 steps in 3 phases, with **each step containing its own sub-framework**, is structurally deeper than any other coach. Most coaches stop at the top-level stage. Fletcher nests inner frameworks inside each step.

### 8. Buyer-centric vs seller-centric stage design

From the broader B2B pipeline literature:

> "The foundation of a high-performing pipeline is stage design that reflects what the buyer has **decided**, not what the seller has **done**."

Buyer-centric stages like *Problem Confirmed* or *Evaluation Criteria Agreed* beat seller-centric stages like *Proposal Sent* or *Demo Scheduled*. Most of these coaches lean buyer-centric in their philosophy, even if their stage labels don't always reflect it.
