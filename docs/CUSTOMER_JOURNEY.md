# Customer Journey

The **Customer Journey** is the long track every deal runs on, from first lead to repeat purchase.

This is one of two independent tools in the ADGA deal system. The other is the **Call Flow** (`CALL_FLOW.md`), which structures a single sales call. The two tools are separate, surfaced in different places, and must not be merged.

## Phase Summary

The customer journey has **3 phases**, **9 stages**, and **9 sub-steps inside each stage** (81 total). This deep, multi-layer structure gives every stage its own internal progression — so the platform can guide an owner through what to do next at any point in any deal.

| Phase | Transformation | Stages |
|---|---|---|
| **Acquire** | Cold → Qualify | Lead · Qualify · Discover |
| **Monetize** | Qualify → Commitment | Scope · Design · Close |
| **Grow** | Commitment → Customer | Sign · Deliver · Expand |

## Where the Customer Journey surfaces

- **Pipeline view** — kanban columns equal the 9 stages, grouped visibly by phase (Acquire · Monetize · Grow)
- **Deal record** — stage indicator, sub-step progress, stage confidence, next action, owner
- **Deal mindmap** — every touchpoint, call, document, artifact, and person attaches to the deal node
- **Reports** — conversion-by-stage and conversion-by-sub-step charts use these exact transitions

## Mindmap binding

Every Customer Journey artifact lives on the deal mindmap. The mindmap is the visual source of truth for a single deal.

Bound entities:

- Contacts, companies, represented clients
- Calls (with brief, summary, transcript)
- Documents (proposals, contracts, invoices, presentations, scope sheets)
- Tasks, commitments, blockers, decisions
- Touchpoints (emails, SMS, voice notes, meetings, calendar holds)
- Approvals (pending, granted, rejected — with grace-window status)

Mindmap surface requirements:

- **Left-side palette** of draggable elements: new contact, new task, new document slot, new call placeholder, new commitment, new decision
- **Drag-and-drop** onto the canvas attaches the element to the deal record automatically and persists to the deal timeline
- **Document import** — documents and presentations created elsewhere can be dropped onto the mindmap and bound to the deal
- **Bidirectional binding** — every node on the mindmap traces back to a record; every record reflects on the mindmap when a relationship exists

## Contacts vs Customers

The CRM surface separates two record types:

| Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Contact** | Any person in the system — leads, prospects, partners, vendors, references, internal stakeholders |
| **Customer** | A contact (or company) that has reached **Sign** on at least one Customer Journey and made a payment |

A contact becomes a customer automatically when the first deal they are attached to reaches Sign. The customer record carries: lifetime value, deal history, expansion paths, satisfaction signal, renewal date, support route, relationship owner.

Both lists surface the standard CRM columns: name, company, owner, last touch, next action, stage, value, source, tags.

## Acquire path (sub-step 8 of Expand)

Acquire lives inside **Expand** as one of the growth paths (alongside renewal, upsell, cross-sell, referral, partner transition). This preserves the 3-phase × 9-stage structure. If the acquisition motion proves to be a distinct enough workload that the same agent cannot own both, it gets promoted to a 10th stage later.

## Public copy rules

Use:

- Customer Journey, the nine-stage spine
- Lead through expand
- Stage names (Lead, Qualify, Discover, Scope, Design, Close, Sign, Deliver, Expand)
- Phase names (Acquire, Monetize, Grow) when grouping is useful

Avoid:

- Any reference to the Call Flow beats or its name — the Call Flow is a separate, internal tool
- Old eight-stage names (Signal, Capture, Shape, Advance) — outdated
- Internal agent names
- Behind-the-scenes automation mechanics
- Long trial framing or "free trial" as primary CTA
