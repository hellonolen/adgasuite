01 · Coach

Aaron Fletcher

Founder of Fletcher Method, Inc. Built a 9-step online marketing system organized in three phases. Over 6,000 entrepreneurs have used it.

Background Spent ~15 years working with large Silicon Valley brands (Nolo, Caring) and small business marketing before launching Fletcher Method in 2012.

The 9 Steps in 3 Phases

PhaseTheme#StepWhat it does
FoundationAnonymous → Intimate1AvatarFind the ideal customer
2MessageMillion Dollar Message — one powerful sentence
3OfferProduct Ladder — map pricing across products
Sales FunnelLeaky Bucket → Airtight4Signature SolutionVisual representation of your unique process
5Swim Lanes Funnel5-Minute Funnel Finder

Key Concepts

  • Frameworks as currency — "An ever-growing repository of one-page cheat sheets that cut out 95% of the time and frustration."
  • Sequence matters — "The power of frameworks lies in completing them accurately and in order."
  • Nested sub-frameworks — Each of the 9 steps contains its own internal framework.
  • Authority Amplifier — Bridge framework between Lead Magnet and conversion event.
02 · Coach

Ryan Deiss

Founder of DigitalMarketer. Creator of "Customer Value Optimization." Built an 8-stage customer journey for moving customers from a less desirable BEFORE state to a more desirable AFTER state.

Background Inspired in part by Desmond Morris (12 stages of human courtship); Deiss condensed it to 8 for marketing.

The 8 Stages

#StageWhat happens
1AwareFirst discovery — ads, blogs, social, word-of-mouth
2EngageBuild connection through helpful content
3SubscribeTrade something valuable for their email
4ConvertSmall first action — trial, sample, entry offer ($8–$20)
5ExciteDopamine hit — memorable experience, quick wins
6AscendMove up the value ladder — where real profit happens
7AdvocateAsk for reviews, testimonials — passive promotion
8PromoteActive promotion — affiliate, referral, true belief

Phase Grouping

  • Acquire — Aware, Engage, Subscribe
  • Monetize — Convert, Excite
  • Maximize — Ascend, Advocate, Promote
"No matter what you're selling, your product or service is a vehicle that transports people from a less desirable before state to a more desirable after state."

— Ryan Deiss

05 · Coach

Donald Miller

Author of Building a StoryBrand. Founder of StoryBrand and Business Made Simple. Built the framework on the hero's journey — a messaging structure that makes the customer the hero and the brand the guide.

The 7 Parts

#PartWhat it means
1CharacterThe hero is your customer, not your brand
2ProblemExternal (surface) + Internal (feeling) + Philosophical ("this shouldn't be this hard")
3GuideYou are the guide — empathy + authority
4PlanSimple 3-step plan that makes saying yes easy
5Call to ActionPrimary ("Schedule a Call") + Secondary ("Download the Guide")
6Avoid FailureStakes — what they lose if they don't act
7SuccessPaint the after-picture — what life looks like solved

Paradigm Shift

The customer is the hero. Your brand is the guide. Most brands position themselves as the hero, which is a major mistake.

"Never assume people understand how your brand can change their lives. Tell them."

— Donald Miller

06 · Coach

Chet Holmes

Author of The Ultimate Sales Machine. Trained Fortune 500 sales teams. Built the Buyer's Pyramid and Stadium Pitch frameworks.

Updated by Amanda Holmes (CEO of Chet Holmes International) introduced the Inverted Buyer's Pyramid in the updated edition.

The Buyer's Pyramid

At any given moment, in any market:

%State
3%Buying now
7%Open to buying
30%Not thinking about it
30%Don't think they're interested
30%Definitely not interested

Data was empirically derived: Chet polled audiences of thousands at live events.

The Stadium Pitch

Imagine all your leads in a stadium. If you talk about your product, 90% would leave. The Stadium Pitch gets the entire pyramid to stay by leading with education-based marketing, not sales pitches.

❌ Bad✅ Good
"The 5 ways our office equipment can benefit you" — appeals to 10%"The 5 ways you're wasting money in operations" — keeps the whole stadium

The Inverted Buyer's Pyramid

  1. Global Pain — Universal pain that catches the entire pyramid
  2. Targeted Pain — Then narrow to your specific problem domain

Red Ocean / Blue Ocean

  • Red Ocean — the 3% buying now (everyone fighting for them)
  • Blue Ocean — the 97% who are latent (open to education)
07 · Coach

Jay Abraham

One of the world's most renowned business strategists. Framework first articulated in the 1980s. Underpins many modern coaching/consultancy frameworks.

The 3 Pillars

Every business grows in exactly three ways. Pull all three levers and growth becomes geometric, not linear.

#LeverWhat it does
1Increase the number of clientsNew customer acquisition
2Increase the average transaction valueEach customer buys more per checkout
3Increase the frequency of purchasesExisting customers buy more often
"Do any one and you can grow in a linear manner... But when you leverage all 3 at the same time, the business growth will be explosive."

— Jay Abraham

If you improve each pillar by 10%, the combined effect is multiplicative, not additive. Most businesses obsess over Pillar 1 — the most expensive and least leveraged path. The breakthrough is dedicating equal attention to Pillars 2 and 3.

08 · Coach

Frank Kern

Direct-response marketer. Creator of Behavioral Dynamic Response (BDR) and Intent Based Branding (IBB).

Behavioral Dynamic Response

An automated marketing method that customizes messaging based on the prospect's behavior, instead of putting everyone on the same conveyor belt.

  • Profit from the No — many prospects won't buy first time; use trust-building over time
  • Response Indicators — specific actions (started video, finished video, clicked link) that tell you where they are
  • Customized Messaging — each behavior triggers a different, targeted follow-up

Intent Based Branding

A method to make prospects know, like, and trust your business before you ever try to sell. Trust is the bridge between product and customer.

The 4-Stage Trust Cycle

Know → Like → Trust → Buy

Industry Frameworks

Non-coach frameworks that informed the research — established sales methodologies and CRM standards.

Sandler Selling System (7 Phases)

  1. Bonding and rapport
  2. Up-front contract
  3. Pain
  4. Budget
  5. Decision
  6. Solution
  7. Post-Sale

Key concept: Sandler flips the traditional model — the buyer pursues the deal. Strong focus on qualifying out quickly via the "pain funnel."

MEDDIC / MEDDPICC

A qualification checklist for enterprise deals:

  • Metrics · Economic Buyer · Decision Criteria · Decision Process
  • Identify Pain · Champion · Paper Process · Competition

Challenger Sale (5 Phases)

  1. Warm-up
  2. Reframe
  3. Rational drowning
  4. Emotional impact
  5. Solution

HubSpot Flywheel

Attract → Engage → Delight. Replaces the funnel metaphor with a compounding wheel.

Universal CRM Lifecycle

The standard record-type sequence across Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, Attio:

Lead → Opportunity → Customer

Standard B2B Sales Pipeline (9 stages)

Prospecting → Qualification → Discovery → Demo → Proposal → Negotiation → Closing → Implementation → Retention

Field Observations

Patterns and observations across the field, drawn from the research.

Observation 01

Only Deiss built a literal stage sequence

Of all 7 coaches, only Ryan Deiss published a stage-by-stage customer journey with named customer states. The others built different shapes — pricing architectures, lead acquisition models, messaging frameworks, market awareness models, growth levers, behavioral methodologies, and coaching curricula.

Observation 02

Consumer info-product orientation

Most of these frameworks were built for consumer info-products — courses, books, coaching programs, software. They assume short sales cycles, single decision-makers, fixed-price offers, and a "click and buy" conversion event.

Observation 03

No coach in this group built a contract-signing stage

Consumer info-product journeys assume Convert = transaction = done. There is no separation between verbal commitment and a signed contract because consumers don't sign contracts to buy a $97 product. None of these coaches designed for that gap.

Observation 04

No coach in this group built a scoping stage

Consumer products are pre-built. There is no negotiated scope. Brunson's Frontend is the closest, but it's a fixed price-point offer, not custom-scoped work.

Observation 05

Post-sale is where most coaches expand

Deiss splits post-sale into 3 stages. Brunson splits it into 3 rungs. Hormozi has 2 money phases. They all see post-sale as where the real money is, and they all break it into multiple stages.

Observation 06

The 3-phase shape is universal

Almost every coach uses some version of this 3-phase grouping: Phase 1 get them in · Phase 2 get them to buy · Phase 3 keep them buying. The names differ; the shape is the same.

Observation 07

The Fletcher pattern is the deepest structural innovation

Fletcher's 9 steps in 3 phases, with each step containing its own sub-framework, is structurally deeper than any other coach in this group. Most coaches stop at the top-level stage. Fletcher nests inner frameworks inside each step.

Observation 08

Buyer-centric vs seller-centric stage design

"The foundation of a high-performing pipeline is stage design that reflects what the buyer has decided, not what the seller has done."

Buyer-centric stages like Problem Confirmed or Evaluation Criteria Agreed beat seller-centric stages like Proposal Sent or Demo Scheduled. Most of these coaches lean buyer-centric in their philosophy, even if their stage labels don't always reflect it.
