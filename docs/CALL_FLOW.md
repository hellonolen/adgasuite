# Call Flow

The **Call Flow** is the structure of a single sales call — minutes long. It defines what happens beat-by-beat from the moment someone picks up to the moment a verbal yes is captured and paperwork queues.

This is one of two independent tools in the ADGA deal system. The other is the **Customer Journey** (`CUSTOMER_JOURNEY.md`), which is the long track every deal runs on, from first lead to repeat purchase. The two tools are separate. The Call Flow runs *inside* certain Customer Journey stages, but it is not the inner framework of those stages — they live side by side.

## Compact View — The 9 Beats

| # | Beat |
|---|---|
| 1 | Connect |
| 2 | Why |
| 3 | Situation |
| 4 | Tried |

| 7 | Close |

| 9 | Sign |

## Phase Summary

The Call Flow has **3 phases**, **9 beats**, and **9 sub-steps inside each beat** (81 total micro-actions). This deep, multi-layer structure gives every beat its own internal progression — so the operator running the call has a clear next move at any moment.

| Phase | Transformation | Beats |
|---|---|---|
| **Open** | Cold → Trust | Connect · Why · Situation |
| **Diagnose** | Trust → Tension | Tried · The wall · Future state |
| **Commit** | Tension → Yes | Close · Investment · Sign |

## The Two Closing Questions (Beat 7 — Close)

These two questions land at the heart of the Close beat. Both are asked, in order, with full silence after each.

1. *"Is there any reason we shouldn't work together?"*
2. *"If there's something that needs to happen between now and you reaching success based on what you've shared, what does that look like? I can help you with that."*

## What was merged from the original 11 beats

The Call Flow began as 11 beats: Connect → Why → Situation → Tried → Ready → The wall → Future state → Close → Investment → Commitment → Sign. We collapsed it to 9 to match the 3 × 3 structure:

- **Ready** (old beat 5) → becomes the **gate between Diagnose and Commit**. If they're not ready, the call ends in Diagnose and the lead routes back to Qualify on the Customer Journey. It's a checkpoint, not a standalone beat.
- **Commitment + Sign** (old beats 10 + 11) → collapsed into the single **Sign** beat. Verbal yes and ink are mechanically tied; one beat captures both.

## Objection philosophy

Objections are not handled — they signal the lead is not ready. If real objections surface in Commit, Diagnose failed earlier. The lead returns to the queue on the Customer Journey with a follow-up scheduled, not pressure to overcome the objection.

The Call Flow handles **questions**, not objections. Questions get answered. Objections route back to Qualify.

## Where the Call Flow runs

The Call Flow is invoked during four Customer Journey stages:

| Customer Journey Stage | Call Flow Invocation | Call Type |
|---|---|---|
| Qualify (stage 2) | Open → Diagnose, gate at Ready | Qualifying call |
| Discover (stage 3) | Open → Diagnose, ends at Future state | Discovery call |
| Scope (stage 4) | Open → Diagnose with scope confirmation | Tech call |
| Close (stage 6) | Full 9-beat flow, ending in Sign | Closing call |

Each stage that uses the Call Flow opens with a **Call Brief** (auto-prepared from prior touches) and closes with a **Call Summary** (filled from transcript, edited by the human).

## Visibility

The Call Flow is an **internal product capability**. Public marketing may reference that a guided call flow exists inside the product, but must not enumerate the beats or expose the framework. This is methodology — kept inside the product, not on marketing pages.

## Origin

The 9-beat structure was collapsed from an earlier 11-beat version to match the 3 × 3 phase structure. The two closing questions and the strict ordering of beats are ADGA-original — built specifically for the moment between emotional commit and price reveal.
