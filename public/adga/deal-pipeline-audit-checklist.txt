Five Secrets to Not Losing Million-Dollar Deals

1. Control the problem before you present the solution.
Million-dollar deals are lost when the seller moves faster than the buyer's real concern. Before you pitch, confirm the problem in the buyer's words.

2. Map the decision path, not just the decision-maker.
One champion is not the same as a closed deal. Know who can approve, who can delay, and who can quietly kill the deal.

3. Never leave the next move implied.
Momentum stalls when the next step lives in somebody's memory. Give the next action an owner, a date, and a reason.

4. Attach proof before doubt appears.
Trust is built before the objection is spoken. Give the other side the proof they need to feel safe.

5. Remove friction from the close path.
A deal is not closed because everyone feels good. Terms, signature, invoice, payment, onboarding, and handoff need to be visible before the close.
